### Question 1
Traditional apps like Instagram store your data on company servers, can ban your account, and change terms of service unilaterally—you're a user of their platform, not an owner of your data. What fundamentally makes an application "decentralized" versus a traditional app?

- A) Being available on mobile app stores in multiple countries worldwide
- B) Running on blockchain where no single entity controls the code, data, or user access ✓
- C) Having servers distributed across multiple data centers for redundancy
- D) Being developed by a team located in different geographic regions

> **Rationale:** The correct answer is B. Decentralization means no single point of control—code runs on blockchain, users control their keys, and no company can unilaterally censor or change rules. Option A describes distribution, not decentralization. Option C is redundancy, not decentralization. Option D is about team structure, irrelevant to app architecture.
